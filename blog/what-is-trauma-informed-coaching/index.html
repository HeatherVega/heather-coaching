Trauma

What is trauma-informed coaching —
and how is it different from therapy?

People ask this a lot. The honest answer matters — especially if you're deciding what kind of support you actually need.

When people find their way to integration coaching, they often come with a version of this question: Is this therapy? Do I need therapy instead? How do I know what I actually need?

These are good questions, and I want to answer them honestly — not to draw a clean line, but because understanding the difference helps you make a better decision for yourself.

"Therapy treats. Coaching accompanies. Both can be part of a full life."

What trauma-informed means

Trauma-informed doesn't mean we spend our sessions excavating your past. It means the work is conducted with an awareness of how trauma lives in the body and shapes present experience. It means we don't push past capacity. We track what's happening somatically, not just cognitively. We work at a pace that the nervous system can tolerate — not the pace the mind wishes it could tolerate.

It also means I understand that what looks like resistance is often protection. And that the goal is never to override that protection, but to work with it until safety emerges naturally.

How coaching and therapy differ

Therapy — particularly with a licensed trauma therapist — is the appropriate place to process and treat clinical trauma, PTSD, and serious mental health conditions. A therapist is trained to diagnose and treat. The work often involves going into difficult material with clinical skill and care.

Integration coaching is different in scope and purpose. We're not treating; we're building. The orientation is forward-facing: What are you moving toward? What practices and frameworks support your nervous system? How do you translate what you've seen and felt into a life that actually reflects it? If clinical care is what you need, I'll say so — and I'll help you find it.

When coaching is the right fit

Coaching works well when you've had a significant experience — a retreat, a ceremony, a loss, a threshold — and you want support integrating what arose. When you're fundamentally functional and want to grow, not just stabilize. When you need someone who understands the specific territory of psychedelic integration and nervous system work, and can meet you there without judgment.
